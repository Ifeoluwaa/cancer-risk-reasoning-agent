# Cancer Risk Reasoning Agent (CRRA)

## Vision

Cancer Risk Reasoning Agent (CRRA) is a multi-agent AI system designed to help users understand the factors that contribute to future cancer risk through evidence-based reasoning.

Unlike traditional healthcare AI systems that focus on diagnosis, prediction, or treatment recommendations, CRRA focuses on explanation and transparency. The system separates evidence retrieval, causal reasoning, counterfactual analysis, and scientific skepticism into specialized agents that collaborate to produce a balanced and interpretable assessment.

The goal is not to tell users whether they will develop cancer, but to help them understand:

* Which risk factors may be most relevant to them
* What scientific evidence supports those factors
* How lifestyle or genetic changes could alter the reasoning
* Where uncertainty exists
* What additional information may be needed

By combining evidence-grounded retrieval, multi-agent collaboration, and explicit uncertainty reporting, CRRA aims to demonstrate a safer and more trustworthy approach to AI-assisted health reasoning.

## Core Principles

### Evidence First

All conclusions should be supported by retrieved evidence from trusted sources.

### Reasoning Over Prediction

The system explains contributors and relationships rather than predicting outcomes.

### Transparency

Users should be able to understand how conclusions were reached.

### Scientific Skepticism

The system should actively challenge its own conclusions and surface uncertainty.

### Safety

The system must never diagnose cancer, predict cancer probability, prescribe treatment, or replace professional medical advice.
